PillPack is a full service pharmacy that delivers a better, simpler experience through convenient packaging, modern technology, and personalized service. We are using design, service, and technology to change the way people think about medicine. Grove is leading the movement toward sustainable, diversified agriculture with a range of products and technologies that empower people to grow and source fresh, local food. Designed and built in Massachusetts, Grove’s intelligent, indoor gardening appliances educate and inspire people to eat healthier, live sustainably and be more self sufficient.

Our appliances are all web connected and come with an awesome web/mobile app, so there's all sorts of awesome engineering to do! From manufacturing to mechanical to fluids to embedded systems to LED power + control, to web + app dev (we run on Node.js & Meteor!) we are a true technology company with a vision for making the world a healthier and more sustainable. We love Olin and have been blown away by the talent and passion emanating from your school! See you all at the fair!

Onshape is the first and only full-cloud 3D CAD system that lets everyone on a design team simultaneously work together using a web browser, phone or tablet.

Onshape is a new venture-backed company that is applying cloud, web and mobile technologies to CAD. Our team includes the original SolidWorks team plus elite engineers from the data center, security, and mobile industries. We are working hard on a new generation of CAD.

MarkForged is a startup that enables 3D printing of parts stronger than metal. Our first product, the Mark One, is the world's first 3D printer that prints carbon fiber, Kevlar, and fiberglass, and is already shipping. The Mark One is powered by Eiger: the world's first WebGL powered 3D printing software that lives entirely within the browser. Come see them both in action at our booth or get in touch with us at jobs@mark-forged.com.

Co-founded in 2010 by 2 MIT professors, CMT develops incentive-based mobile apps for safe driving that are being used worldwide, with aims of making drivers better and roads safer. CMT also develops a hardware product that attaches to car windshields and shares acceleration data with CMT's mobile apps as part of a product-service package for insurance companies. CMT has about 20 employees and is based in Kendall Square.

Bevi is a venture-backed, fast-growing start-up building a smarter, more environmental platform for beverage delivery. Instead of wasting bottles and cans, we make internet-controlled machines that purify water and mix a variety of drinks at the point of use. Our first product is a custom beverage machine for offices.

Affectly is an automated donor engagement platform for data-driven online fundraising. In 30 days on the platform, the average Affectly user increases their social media following by 137% and drive 3X the industry average number of new donors to a fundraising drive. he platform uses data science and machine learning models to allow nonprofits to activate influential supporters, target new donors, and build meaningful relationships with their advocates by tracking the impact of actions taken on the organizations behalf. Affectly's mission is to inspire a lifetime of generosity by studying the science of why we “do good.”

Energy Intelligence: Generating Electricity From the Motion of Vehicles Energy Intelligence is creating a powerful new source of clean energy at high-traffic facilities by leveraging an untapped resource: Cars! The company has patented and developed an innovative system that lies flat on roads at parking garages, border crossings, and truck stops, and harnesses the kinetic energy from braking vehicles to power equipment onsite and reduce the facility’s electricity expenses up to 50%. Energy Intelligence will own the rugged, durable, and modular systems, which can be easily installed without road excavation, and will sell the electricity generated behind the meter to site owners. Moreover, with wireless sensors and data collection, this smart technology modernizes our cities and improves sustainability. Energy Intelligence expanded into Buffalo in late 2014 upon winning the 43North competition and has made tremendous progress since, including: Growing the team in Buffalo, receiving a third issued patent, partnering with the Buffalo Niagara Medical Campus, National Grid, and The Peace Bridge Authority, deploying it’s first complete field test, and most recently winning Steve Case’s Rise of the Rest and a $100k top prize.
